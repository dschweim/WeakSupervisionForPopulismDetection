Miliband desperate to distance himself from Brown on Iraq says Davey

Mon, 19 Apr 2010

With Labour divided all the way to the top over Iraq, its time they and the Conservatives apologised to the British public for the worst foreign policy disaster since Suez," said the Liberal Democrat Shadow Foreign Secretary.

Commenting on David Milibands claim that Britain would not have invaded Iraq had it been known that the country did not have WMD, Edward Davey said:

This is a desperate attempt by David Miliband to distance himself from Gordon Browns justification for the Iraq war.

With Labour divided all the way to the top over Iraq, its time they and the Conservatives apologised to the British public for the worst foreign policy disaster since Suez.
